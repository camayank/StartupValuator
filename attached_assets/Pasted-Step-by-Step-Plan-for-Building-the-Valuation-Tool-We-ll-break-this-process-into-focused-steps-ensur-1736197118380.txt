Step-by-Step Plan for Building the Valuation Tool
We'll break this process into focused steps, ensuring depth and precision at each stage. Let’s start with Step 1: UI/UX Design Prototypes, followed by backend architecture, and so on.

Step 1: UI/UX Design Prototypes
Objective: Create a user interface that is intuitive, visually appealing, and purpose-driven for minimal user effort while ensuring maximum functionality.

1. Landing Page Prototype
Purpose: First impressions matter. Showcase the tool's capabilities and drive users to sign up or start a valuation immediately.

Hero Section:

Headline: "Your Business, Your Value, Instantly."
Subtext: "Unlock your startup’s potential with professional-grade valuations, AI-driven insights, and global compliance."
CTA Buttons: "Start Valuation", "Learn More", "Sign In/Sign Up".
Key Features Section:

Icons + Text:
"AI-Powered Insights for Accurate Valuations."
"Compliance-Ready Reports for Every Jurisdiction."
"Fast, Easy, and Affordable Valuation for All Businesses."
Social Proof:

Testimonials from early adopters or mock reviews.
Logos of accelerators or VCs as partners.
2. Login/Signup Screen
Options:

Google login for speed.
Email-based signup with password and OTP verification.
Post-Signup:

Brief onboarding steps with tool highlights:
“Start by entering your business details.”
3. Workflow Navigation
Design: Card-based navigation with clear progress indicators (e.g., 20%, 40% complete).
Steps:

Business Information
Industry Variables
Financial Information
Valuation Method
Review and Report
4. Data Entry Screens
Business Information:

Inputs: Name, purpose, region, stage.
Tooltip: Explain the importance of each field.
Industry Variables:

Dropdown to select the industry and dynamic inputs appear based on the selection.
Example (SaaS):
Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR).
Churn Rate.
Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC).
Financial Information:

Inputs: Annual Revenue, Growth Rate, Margins.
Slider-Based Inputs:
Growth Rate (0%-100%).
Margins (0%-100%).
5. Report Customization Screen
Options for Branding:

Upload logo.
Choose report format (PDF, Excel).
Visual Adjustments:

Select chart types for presentation (bar, pie, line).
6. Dashboard
Purpose: Allow users to save and revisit past valuations.
Features:
Saved Reports.
Subscription Details.
Quick Start Valuation Button.
